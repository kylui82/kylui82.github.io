ROLE
Product Designer
SKILL
UI/UX Design
TOOL
Figma, Zeplin

Problem

Lack of features

The original workforce management system was built by developers. It only consisted 4 buttons at the landing pages and did't have much functionalities for users.

Outdated design

Our team thought the UI design was outdated and not visually appealing to users.

Brainstorm

Our user segementations are Store manager, Store associate, HR manager for managing multiple stores. Our team was trying to think about the user stories during the brainstorming stage in order to address the problems. The user stories helped to define the functionality and features of a workforce management system from the perspective of various users.


Persona

Some of the User Stories

As an Employee, I want to submit time-off requests through the system, including the option to select specific dates, provide a reason, and track the approval status, so I can easily manage my time off.

As an Employee, I want to receive notifications about my upcoming shifts and any changes to my schedule in real-time, so I can plan my personal life around work commitments.

As a Manager, I want to have a dashboard displaying employee attendance, so I can make decisions to optimize workforce management.

As an Employee, I want to update my personal information, such as address, emergency contacts, and tax withholding preferences, to keep my records accurate.


Moodboard

I created mood boards to collect visual inspiration by researching other Workforce Management Solution, and decided on the product's visual direction.

Wireframe

Create low-fidelity wireframes to sketch out the basic layout and flow of the UI.

Icon Design

Trying to build a set of icons for universal usage.

Design

I chose a blue color scheme, typography, and visual style that align with our company's brand and created a professional and cohesive look. Also, I designed UI elements, including buttons, forms, icons, and graphics, to ensure consistency and usability.
